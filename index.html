The Life and Career of General Sir Hubert Gough

The Generals of the Great War
Haig Rawlinson Plumer Smith-Dorian

Welcome to a brief look at the life of Sir Hubert Gough, the youngest British general during the Great War. While highly respected prior to the outbreak of the Great War, events in the Spring of 1918 would lead his unfair dismissal and a tumultuous subsequent career. Recent historical reexaminations of this period however have done much to exonerate Gough for the failure of the 5th army to stand its ground in the face of one of the largest offensive operations of the war. By following the links below, you will find more information about one of the most interesting figures of the 1914-18 conflict.

While seldom remembered today, Gough is often considered to be the 'right man in the wrong war', his style of positional warfare and aggressive action being better suited to the realities of the battlefield in the Second World War rather than the First. His legacy is complex and still contested among both supporters and detractors alike.
